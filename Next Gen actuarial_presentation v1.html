Deep Dive: Credit Data & Mortality
BEHAVIORAL PROXIES
CREDIT ANALYTICS
Observed Relationship
  • Strong empirical correlation between credit stability and mortality outcomes
  • Credit data acts as a proxy for lifestyle, stress, and access to care
Use Cases
  • Enhancing mortality assumption setting
  • Improving underwriting triage (simplified vs. full UW)
  • Supporting experience study segmentation
Key Pitfalls
  • Correlation ≠ causation — credit is a proxy, not a biological driver
  • Regulatory sensitivity around fairness and indirect discrimination
  • Data drift as macroeconomic conditions change
Proxy Risk Data Drift Reg. Sensitivity
Actuarial Guardrails
  • Use credit variables as supporting factors, not sole drivers
  • Continuous monitoring and periodic re-calibration
  • Clear documentation of intent and limitations
  • Adverse impact ratio testing pre-production
  • Fallback mechanisms where credit data unavailable
Key Research Findings
Harvard / ACS Study (2025)
Cancer patients with a 2-tier credit score drop saw 29% higher mortality. A 6-month 2-tier drop → 63% higher mortality.
RGA Validation (2023)
Milliman RxDxCr3.0 (Rx + medical + credit) is the most powerful combined score. Credit captures orthogonal risk dimension.
SOA / Academic Research
Credit scores contain significant info not in traditional rating variables; explained by risk-taking behavior theory.
Key Considerations
  • Proxy Discrimination — Credit, geography, or occupation may correlate with protected classes (race, income). Disparate impact testing is essential.
  • Genetic Information — Moratorium/restrictions on genetic testing results in many jurisdictions. PRS is an emerging gray area.
  • Consent & Transparency — Regulators are developing guidance on AI/ML use in underwriting decisions; insurers should document intent, testing methodology and limitations.
05 / 07
Machine Learning for Experience Studies
AI / ML METHODS
EXPLAINABILITY
Traditional Approach
  • Actual-to-Expected (A/E) ratios against reference tables
  • Segment by single variable at a time
  • Aggregate heterogeneous segments for credibility
  • Linear assumptions between risk factors
Limited to 5–8 risk dimensions
Next-Gen Approach
  • Gradient-boosted trees (XGBoost, LightGBM)
  • Survival analysis (Cox PH, Kaplan-Meier, DeepSurv)
  • Bayesian inference for uncertainty & sparse data
  • Capture non-linear interactions across 100+ features
Discover hidden risk factors & interaction effects
Where ML Adds Value
  • Detects non-linear interactions across hundreds of variables
  • Improves predictive accuracy vs. traditional GLMs
  • Enables faster, more frequent experience refreshes
Typical Applications
  • Mortality curve fitting
  • Credibility weighting across segments
  • Early detection of experience deterioration
Key Pitfalls
  • "Black box" models unacceptable for audit and regulators
  • Overfitting to historical noise
  • Loss of actuarial intuition if models blindly trusted
Role of SHAP & Explainable AI
  • SHAP values decompose predictions into factor-level contributions
  • Enables regulatory defensibility
  • Clear communication to stakeholders
  • Validation under ASOP 56 and OSFI E-23
  • Generates compliant adverse action notices
