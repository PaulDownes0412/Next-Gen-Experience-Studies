Section 1

The Case for Change

Traditional experience studies rely on a narrow set of risk factors that leave significant mortality variation unexplained.

📋 Traditional Factors

  • • Age & gender (male/female)
  • • Smoker vs non-smoker status
  • • Sum assured / face amount band
  • • Policy duration (select vs ultimate)
  • • Underwriting class (preferred, standard, substandard)

🔬 What's Missing

  • • Prescription drug history & medical claims
  • • Financial stability / credit attributes
  • • Lifestyle indicators (activity, sleep, BMI)
  • • Social determinants (education, income, geography)
  • • Non-linear interactions between factors

Key Research Findings

Harvard / ACS Study (2025)

Cancer patients with a 2-tier credit score drop saw 29% higher mortality. A 6-month 2-tier drop → 63% higher mortality.

Section 4

ML for Experience Studies

🔄 Traditional Approach

• Actual-to-Expected (A/E) ratios against reference tables

• Segment by single variable at a time

• Aggregate heterogeneous segments for credibility

• Linear assumptions between risk factors

Limited to 5-8 risk dimensions

🚀 Next-Gen Approach

• Gradient-boosted trees (XGBoost, LightGBM)

• Survival analysis (Cox PH, Kaplan-Meier, DeepSurv)

• Bayesian inference for uncertainty & sparse data

• Capture non-linear interactions across 100+ features

Discover hidden risk factors & interaction effects

SOA "Beyond Actual to Table" (2018): Demonstrated that modeling experience (rather than relying on A/E ratios alone) addresses distortion from mixing heterogeneous segments. Interprets model residuals instead of raw ratios — a foundational step toward ML-driven studies.

Explainability

Explainable AI (XAI)

Black-box models won't fly with regulators or with actuarial professional standards. XAI bridges the gap.

📊

SHAP Values

Game-theory approach assigning each feature an importance value. Shows global feature importance + local per-prediction explanations.

🔍

LIME

Locally Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations. Generates human-readable explanations for individual risk assessments.

🌲

Partial Dependence

PDPs and ICE plots show how mortality varies as a single feature changes — while controlling for all others.

IMPACT Framework (Nature, 2022): Used XAI to explain tree ensemble mortality models on 47,261 NHANES samples with 151 features. Identified overlooked risk factors, non-linear relationships, and suggested adjusting established clinical reference intervals.

Section 5

Regulatory & Fairness

🇨🇦 Canadian Landscape

PIPEDA: Federal privacy law governs collection/use of credit info. Consent required. Ontario Consumer Reporting Act confirms credit can be disclosed for insurance underwriting.

OSFI: Focuses on policyholder protection and prudent risk management. No specific prohibition on alternative data but expects sound practices.

IBC Code: Code of Conduct for credit use in personal insurance (P&C). Sets consent and transparency guidelines.

⚖️ Key Considerations

Proxy Discrimination: Credit, geography, or occupation may correlate with protected classes (race, income). Disparate impact testing is essential.

Genetic Information: Moratorium/restrictions on genetic testing results in many jurisdictions. PRS is an emerging gray area.

Consent & Transparency: NAIC Accelerated Underwriting Working Group developing regulatory guidance on AI/ML in underwriting decisions.

⚠️ Key Takeaway: Using credit data for experience studies (analysing mortality patterns) is different from using it for individual underwriting decisions. The former carries less regulatory risk but requires careful data governance.

Beyond Mortality

Lapse & Termination

The same principles — alternative data, ML models, XAI — apply directly to policyholder behaviour studies.

📉

Lapse Prediction

Premium affordability (credit data), life events (digital footprints), billing mode, and premium shock all interact non-linearly.

🔄

Anti-Selection

Post-level term mortality deterioration driven by selective lapsation. SOA/SCOR 2021 study confirmed premium increase is key driver.

⚡

Dynamic Underwriting

Continuous risk reassessment using wearables and real-time data streams. Pricing responds to actual behavior, not just point-in-time assessment.

SOA PLT Study (2021): Statistical modeling identified premium increase, attained age, billing type as top predictors of both lapse and mortality deterioration — demonstrating the value of multivariate approaches over single-factor A/E analysis.

Proposed Roadmap for iA

Phase 1 — Discovery (3 months)

Inventory current experience study methodology. Assess data availability (Rx, credit, EHR). Map Canadian regulatory constraints. Engage reinsurer partners (Munich Re, RGA) for validation datasets.

Phase 2 — Pilot (6 months)

Build ML mortality model using internal experience + vendor scores. Compare predictive power vs traditional A/E. Apply SHAP/LIME for interpretability. Test on mortality first, then extend to lapse.

Phase 3 — Integration (6 months)

Embed ML assumptions into valuation, pricing, and product management. Develop governance framework for model monitoring. Establish ongoing model validation cadence.

Phase 4 — Scale (Ongoing)

Extend to group insurance, morbidity, disability. Incorporate wearables and real-time data. Move towards continuous/dynamic underwriting. Share findings with CIA.
